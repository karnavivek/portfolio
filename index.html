My Experience at LSO Workshop, IEOR, IIT Bombay

Recently, I had the opportunity to attend Large Scale Optimization (LSO) Workshop which was held at Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay by IEOR, IITBombay. This Workshop was one of the most engaging & rigorous EVER! Over several intense days, we took a deep dive into the kind of hardcore, mathematical optimization that powers real-world solutions. My passion for the field has been completely rejuvenated. We covered a wide range of advanced topics, including:

- Column Generation & Cutting Plane Methods

- Benders & Dantzig-Wolfe Decomposition

- Valid Inequalities & Lagrangian Relaxations

- Heuristics & Meta-Heuristics

- Bilevel Optimization & Polar Dualities

- Computational Complexity of Optimization Models

- Applications in Real World etc.


This workshop was a fantastic opportunity to explore how these advanced techniques can solve complex integer programming challenges, and I'm excited to apply them in practice (Trust me I will ;).
This wouldn't have been possible without all the accomplished professors teaching us, from all TOP Institutes of our country, from people such as Prof Sachin Jayaswal (Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad), Prof. Ankur Sinha (IIM Ahmedabad), Prof. Yogesh Agarwal (Indian Institute of Management, Lucknow (retd.)), Prof. Ashutosh Mahajan (Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay), Prof. Faiz Hamid (Indian Institute of Technology, Kanpur), Prof. Saurabh Chandra (Indian Institute of Management, Indore), Prof. Sriram Sankaranarayanan (Indian School of Business) and many many more. A huge thank you to the entire organizing team for keeping everything so streamlined and to IIT Bombay for being a gracious host. It was so hectic but I could manage to click pictures with 2 of the professors I mentioned before, Thank you Prof. Sachin Sir, you made decomposition easy for us, & Thank you Prof. Ashutosh Sir for organising and teaching the final session on Quadratic Optimization!
